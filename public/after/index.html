After the Storm

Recovery, safety, and what to do first

⚠ Wait for the official all-clear before going outside

Even after the storm passes, downed power lines, flooded roads, and structural damage make going outside dangerous. Wait for official confirmation from authorities before leaving your shelter.

First Steps After the Storm

  • Wait for the official all-clear from the Government of The Bahamas or DRM Authority
  • Check yourself and family members for injuries — provide first aid as needed
  • Check your home for structural damage before re-entering — look for foundation cracks, leaning walls
  • Stay away from downed power lines — assume all lines are live and deadly
  • Do not enter buildings with visible flooding or gas smells
  • Turn off gas at the main valve if you smell gas; open windows and leave immediately

Check on Your Neighbours

Check on elderly and disabled neighbours after the storm passes — NOAA
  • Knock on doors of elderly, disabled, or isolated neighbours
  • Assist with clearing debris from walkways and entrances
  • Share water, food, and supplies with those who lack them
  • Report any missing persons or serious injuries to authorities immediately

Food Safety

⚠ Discard refrigerator food after 4 hours without power

If your refrigerator has been without power for more than 4 hours, food inside may no longer be safe to eat. When in doubt — throw it out. Foodborne illness can be serious and hospitals may be overwhelmed after a storm.

Refrigerator Rules

  • Refrigerator keeps food safe for 4 hours if kept closed
  • A full freezer holds temperature for 48 hours (24 hours if half-full)
  • Never taste food to decide if it's safe — if in doubt, throw it out

Safe to Eat (if not touched by flood water)

  • Undamaged canned goods (discard swollen, rusted, or dented cans)
  • Sealed packages of crackers, pasta, dry beans
  • Hard cheeses, peanut butter, fruit preserves

Discard Immediately

  • Any food that has an unusual odour, colour, or texture
  • Raw or cooked meat, poultry, fish, and eggs (after 4 hours without power)
  • Anything that touched flood water
  • Opened canned goods

Water Safety & Boil Water Advisories

If a Boil Water Advisory is Issued

  • Bring water to a rolling boil for at least 1 minute before drinking, cooking, or brushing teeth
  • Let boiled water cool in a clean, covered container before using
  • Use bottled water as an alternative if available
  • Do NOT use tap water for ice until the advisory is lifted

When is Tap Water Safe Again?

  • Only when authorities officially lift the boil water advisory
  • Listen to the battery radio for official announcements

Mold Prevention & Cleanup

Act within 24–48 hours

Mold begins growing within 24–48 hours of water damage. Act fast to reduce health risks.

  • Open windows and doors to increase airflow as soon as the storm passes
  • Remove standing water using mops, pumps, or wet-dry vacuums
  • Remove and discard waterlogged carpets, mattresses, and soft furnishings — they cannot be safely dried
  • Scrub mold-affected hard surfaces with a solution of 1 cup bleach per 1 gallon of water
  • Wear gloves, eye protection, and an N95 mask when cleaning mold
  • Do not mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners
  • Dry the area completely and keep it ventilated

Debris Removal Safety

  • Wear heavy gloves, long sleeves, and sturdy boots when clearing debris
  • Never touch or move downed power lines — call BPL immediately
  • Be cautious of nails, broken glass, and sharp metal hidden in debris
  • Check for snakes and insects that may have sheltered in debris piles
  • Do not burn debris — wait for official debris removal collection
⚠ BEWARE: Contractor Fraud After Storms

After hurricanes, unlicensed contractors and scammers target vulnerable homeowners. They demand cash upfront, do poor work or no work at all, then disappear. Protect yourself:

  • Always verify a contractor's licence and insurance before signing anything
  • Never pay the full amount upfront — a deposit of no more than 30% is standard
  • Get at least 3 written quotes before hiring anyone
  • Do not be pressured into signing any document at your door
  • Report suspected scams to Crime Stoppers: (242) 328-8477

Insurance Documentation

  • Photograph all damage thoroughly before any cleanup or repairs begin
  • Make a detailed written list of damaged and destroyed items
  • Keep all receipts for cleanup supplies, hotel stays, and temporary repairs
  • Contact your insurer as soon as possible — do not wait
  • Do not make permanent repairs until your insurance company has inspected the damage
  • Ask your insurer for a written claim number and keep it safe
