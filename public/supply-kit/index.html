Hurricane Supply Kit

DRM Authority — Get Storm Ready

Hurricane preparation is not one-size-fits-all. Involve your entire family in the planning process. Your kit should reflect your household's specific needs.

Home Supply Kit (2-Week Supply)

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day — 14 gallons per person for 2 weeks. Store in clean sealed containers.
  • Food: Non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items for 2 weeks — canned goods, dried beans, pasta, rice, peanut butter
  • Manual can opener — essential if you have canned food
  • Flashlight with extra batteries (LED lasts longer)
  • Battery-powered, solar-powered, or hand-crank radio with extra batteries — your lifeline for storm updates
  • First aid kit — bandages, antiseptic, gauze, pain reliever
  • Medications — 7-day supply minimum, plus all medical devices (inhaler, glucose monitor, etc.)
  • Multi-purpose tool (Swiss Army knife or similar)
  • Small hatchet — for home only, kept in the attic in case you need to escape through the roof during flooding
  • Sanitation and hygiene items: soap, hand sanitiser, disinfectant, toilet paper, feminine hygiene items
  • Copies of personal documents in a waterproof bag: ID, passport, birth certificate, proof of address, insurance policies, medication list
  • Cell phone and backup power bank — fully charged before the storm
  • Family and emergency contact information written on paper
  • Extra cash in small bills — ATMs may be down or inaccessible after the storm

Pack Your Go Bag (3-Day Evacuation Kit)

What is a go bag?

A lightweight backpack or duffel bag, always packed and ready to grab at a moment's notice. Every family member should have one. Pack it in advance — don't wait for a storm warning.

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day, 3-day supply (minimum)
  • Food: Non-perishable, 3-day supply — choose foods that require no cooking or minimal water
  • Manual can opener
  • Flashlight with extra batteries
  • Battery/solar/hand-crank radio
  • Dust mask and face covering
  • Medications — 7-day supply
  • Multi-purpose tool
  • Complete change of clothing and sturdy closed-toe shoes
  • Copies of important documents in a waterproof bag
  • Cash in small bills
  • Cell phone and power bank
  • First aid kit
  • Emergency contact list on paper
  • Sleeping bag or warm blanket per person

Special Needs Considerations

Infants & Young Children

  • Diapers — extra supply
  • Baby formula and food
  • Baby wipes and hand sanitiser
  • Any medications or medical devices
  • Comfort items (blanket, stuffed animal)

Seniors & People with Disabilities

  • Extra medications — at least 7 days
  • Walking aid (cane, walker), hearing aids, eyeglasses
  • Medical equipment and backup power if required
  • Keep emergency medical information on your person
  • Register with local emergency management if you require evacuation assistance

Pets

  • Pet food and water — 7-day supply
  • Pet medications
  • Carrier or crate and leash
  • Vet records and vaccination documents
  • Recent photo of your pet
  • Identify pet-friendly shelters or hotels in advance
