Before the Storm

Prepare now — before a storm is named

⚠ Know your evacuation zone BEFORE the storm hits

Don't wait for a hurricane watch. Learn your zone, plan your route, and designate your shelter now while you have time.

Build Your Supply Kit

Home Supplies (2-week supply)

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day — 14 gallons per person for 2 weeks
  • Food: Non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items (canned goods, dried foods)
  • Manual can opener
  • Flashlight with extra batteries
  • Battery, solar, or hand-crank radio with spare batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Medications: 7-day supply + medical items
  • Multi-purpose tool (e.g. Swiss Army knife)
  • Small hatchet (for home only — escape through roof if flooded)
  • Hygiene items: soap, sanitiser, disinfectant
  • Documents (copies): ID, passport, birth certificate, insurance, deed/lease, medication list
  • Cell phone with backup power bank
  • Cash: ATMs may be down or inaccessible after a storm

Special Needs Considerations

  • Infants: Diapers, formula, baby wipes, hand sanitiser
  • Seniors: Extra medications, cane, eyeglasses, hearing aids
  • Pets: Food, water, medications, carriers, vet records

Pack Your Go Bag (3-day evacuation kit)

A go bag is a lightweight backpack or duffel you grab and go when disaster strikes. Every family member should have one.

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day (3-day supply)
  • Food: 3-day supply of non-perishable items
  • Manual can opener
  • Flashlight with extra batteries
  • Battery/solar/hand-crank radio
  • Dust mask and face covering
  • Medications: 7-day supply
  • Multi-purpose tool
  • Change of clothing and sturdy shoes
  • Copies of important documents in waterproof bag
  • Cash in small bills
  • Cell phone and power bank
  • First aid kit
  • Emergency contact list (written on paper)

Family Evacuation Plan

Create Your Evacuation Plan

  • Know your nearest official shelter and the route to get there
  • Plan to take one car per family to avoid separation en route
  • Keep your fuel tank at least half full during hurricane season
  • Designate who will close windows, lock doors, and unplug appliances
  • Prepare evacuation clothing: long shirt, pants, cap, durable shoes, rain gear
  • Avoid flooded roads and stay away from downed power lines
  • Follow all Government of The Bahamas evacuation orders immediately

Designate a Shelter-at-Home Room

  • Choose a small interior room with few or no windows
  • Room should be above ground level if possible (reduces flood risk)
  • Stock the room with your go bag, first aid kit, and water

Compile Emergency Contacts

  • Write emergency contacts on paper — phones die and data is lost
  • Include: police, fire, DRM Authority, local hospital, family doctor, next-of-kin
  • Store the list both physically (on the wall) and in your phone

Home Preparation

  • Install shutters or plywood on all windows and doors
  • Reinforce your garage door — it's often the weakest point
  • Bring inside or tie down all outdoor furniture, decorations, and trash cans
  • Trim trees and remove dead branches that could become projectiles
  • Clear gutters and drains to prevent flooding from blocked water flow
  • Know how to turn off utilities (gas, water, electricity) at the main switches
  • Charge all devices and power banks before the storm arrives

Insurance Checkup

  • Review your homeowner's or renter's insurance policy now, not after
  • Consider flood insurance — standard policies often exclude flood damage
  • Keep copies of all insurance documents in a waterproof bag in your go bag
  • Take a video walkthrough of your home and belongings — store it in the cloud or email it to yourself
  • Know your insurer's claims hotline number by heart

Secure Your Boat

  • Move your vessel to a safe harbour or inland marina before the storm
  • Use extra dock lines — at least three times the normal amount
  • Remove canvas, covers, and anything that creates wind resistance
  • If hauling the boat, place it well inland on a sturdy cradle
  • Photograph the boat and document its current condition for insurance

Practice Your Plan

Conduct drills with all household members. Make sure everyone knows the shelter room location, where emergency supplies are kept, and all emergency exits.
