Household Emergency Plan

DRM Authority — Get Storm Ready

One of the best ways to keep your family safe during the chaos of a hurricane is to plan ahead. Creating a household emergency plan ensures you and your loved ones can act quickly when disaster strikes. Make a plan with your family today.

Create Your Evacuation Plan

  • Ensure all family members know your nearest shelter and the evacuation route to get there
  • Plan to take one car per family to avoid being separated on the road
  • Always keep your fuel tank at least half full during hurricane season
  • Ahead of time, designate who will close and lock windows and doors, who will unplug electrical devices, and who is responsible for the go bags
  • Set aside evacuation-appropriate clothing: long shirt, pants, cap, durable closed-toe shoes, and rain gear
  • Set aside a go bag of essential items to take to the shelter — see the Supply Kit page for contents
⚠ If an evacuation order is issued — LEAVE IMMEDIATELY

Evacuation orders are issued by the Government of The Bahamas. Do not wait. Gather your family, grab your go bag, and go. Avoid flooded areas and stay clear of downed power lines.

Compile Your Emergency Contacts

Keep a written list of emergency contacts. Store it physically on the wall of your home AND in your phone. Do not rely on memory.

Your contact list should include:

  • Police, Fire, and Emergency Medical Services (911 / 919)
  • DRM Authority: (242) 322-6081
  • Your nearest shelter (name and address)
  • Your local hospital or family doctor
  • Next-of-kin — family member not in your household
  • An out-of-island contact — sometimes it's easier to reach someone far away
  • Your insurance company's claims hotline

Have Alternate Shelters

  • Identify at least two people in different neighbourhoods you can stay with if you cannot go to an official shelter
  • Make sure all family members know these contacts by name and phone number
  • Have a backup plan if your first shelter choice is full or inaccessible

Designate a Shelter-at-Home Room

If evacuation is not possible, designate a safe room in your home before the storm season begins.

  • Choose a small interior room with few or no windows
  • The room should be above ground level if possible — this reduces flood risk
  • Stock the room in advance with your go bag, water, and first aid kit
  • If you have a two-story home, an upper floor interior room is ideal during storm surge events

Practice Your Plan

  • Conduct practice drills with all household members so everyone knows their role
  • Walk through your home and ensure everyone knows the location of emergency supplies, first aid kit, and all exits
  • Make sure all family members can reach the shelter room quickly in the dark
  • Review and update your plan at the start of every hurricane season (June 1)

Stay Informed

Continuously monitor official sources

Follow the DRM Authority's official channels and trusted local news sources for storm alerts, evacuation orders, and all-clear announcements. Keep your battery radio on during and after the storm.

In an Emergency — Know These Rules

  • Stay informed — use local radio and official sources, not social media rumours
  • Stay in touch with your neighbours
  • Know who to call and where to find help
  • Know which local officials and response resources are active
  • Never ignore an official evacuation order
